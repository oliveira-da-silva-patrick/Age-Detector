How it Works

Our AI predicts the ages of people in photos by comparing their faces to hundreds of other images of faces online and finding similarities between them. Since appearance can differ substantially even when people are the same age, we chose to divide people into five distinct age groups rather than classify a specific age. This ensures that our AI can make more accurate predictions. Take a look below to learn about the five age groups we selected!

Baby

The baby age group typically describes the first 1-4 years of a person's life. These are key developmental years where one learns how to walk, speak, express emotion, and other important skills.

Adolescent

The adolescent age group has a broader range and usually includes ages 5-17 or 10-19, depending on the source. These years are characterized by the childhood and teenage years of a person’s life where their brain continues to develop and they go through puberty.  

Young Adult

The young adult age group enco​mpasses the ages 18-25 and describes the period when a person enters legal adulthood but has not yet fully matured. In this period, one gains social responsibility and typically begins to pursue a career or higher education. 

Adult

The adult age group has the largest age range of 25-65 years old and describes the period after the brain has finished developing and the person is fully matured. This is generally the period of life when people settle into a career and/or start a family.

Senior

The ages 65 and older encompass the senior age group. People in this group have hopefully saved enough money to be able to stop working and go into retirement, and have more time to pursue personal interests such as new hobbies.

Resources

Labelbox

Labelbox is a data training platform that allowed us to import and label hundreds of pictures of different age groups. Through their fast and easy labeling system, we were able to quickly assign different values to the images that later informed our AI on which faces belonged to which age groups. 

labelbox

YOLO

YOLO is an object detection algorithm used to detect objects and identify what they are. This system looks at the whole image at test time so its predictions are informed by global context in the image, making it much faster than a convolutional neural network model. Along with Labelbox, this platform allowed us to train our AI relatively quickly.
